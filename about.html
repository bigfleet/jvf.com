A Bit About Me

These days, I say that I’m a freelance CTO. My background is in software development. I did HTML and PHP in high school, then learned Java in college. After being uninspired by what was then the state of the art in Java programming on the web, I left for Ruby and haven’t looked back yet.

At this writing in the July of 2013, I am 34 years old. I grew up in East Tennessee, attending Maryville High School. I went to school at Rice where I help with recruiting. After graduating, I moved to the Richmond, VA area where I met my lovely wife, Megan. We were married on August 18, 2007. We moved to Charlotte, NC, where we share a lovely home just outside I-277, minutes from Uptown. On April 19 of 2011, she gave birth naturally to our son Jefferson James Van Fleet.

After a position at TradeKing brought me to Charlotte, I began freelancing after I left. I was the full-time CEO at OtherScreen, until that company folded, and now I’ve returned to independent work. I have some confidence I’ll remain that way forever, but I suppose it will depend on how good a salesman I can become.

My primary hobbies are basically indistinguishable from my work. My other hobbies, like disc golf, video games, writing and learning to play the guitar, don’t really come up that much. When I have free-time, I am usually supporting the Charlotte startup community in some way.

Credit for my avatar goes to my brother-in-law, Ryan Epp, and Megan, who asked him to do it.

What Now?

If you are a software person, you might want to check out my open-source software contributions. I think it’s more likely you’d want to subscribe to my newsletter about the Charlotte Startup Community or perhaps follow me on Twitter.

I also have walkthroughs for a couple of brain-teasers, the Self-Referential Aptitude Test and Who Owns the Fish?
